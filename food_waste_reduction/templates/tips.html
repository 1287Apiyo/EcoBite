Food Waste Tips

  • Plan Your Meals: Before grocery shopping, plan your meals for the week. Create a shopping list based on your meal plan to avoid buying unnecessary items.
  • Check Your Pantry and Fridge: Before you shop, take inventory of what you already have. Use ingredients that are nearing their expiration dates first.
  • Store Food Properly: Different foods have different storage needs. Learn how to store fruits, vegetables, and leftovers correctly to extend their shelf life.
  • Use Clear Containers: Store food in clear containers so you can easily see what you have. This reduces the chance of forgetting about food that’s about to expire.
  • Understand Expiration Dates: Learn the difference between “best by,” “sell by,” and “use by” dates. Many foods are still safe to eat after these dates.
  • Freeze Surplus Food: If you have food that you won’t be able to eat in time, consider freezing it. Many fruits, vegetables, and leftovers can be safely frozen.
  • Incorporate Leftovers: Get creative with your leftovers. Transform them into new meals or use them in soups, stews, or stir-fries.
  • Share with Friends and Neighbors: If you have excess food that you won’t be able to eat, consider sharing it with friends, family, or neighbors.
  • Compost Food Scraps: Instead of throwing away food scraps, compost them. This not only reduces waste but also enriches your garden soil.
  • Educate Yourself on Portion Sizes: Be mindful of how much food you prepare or order. Knowing portion sizes can help you avoid cooking or buying too much.
  • Repurpose Wilting Vegetables: Use wilting vegetables in stocks, soups, or smoothies instead of discarding them.
  • Cook in Batches: Prepare meals in larger quantities and freeze portions for later. This reduces the likelihood of cooking more food than necessary.
  • Use Produce Before It Spoils: Make a habit of checking your fridge regularly and use fruits and vegetables before they spoil.
  • Join a Food Share Program: Participate in local food share or community garden programs to share excess produce and reduce waste.
  • Get Creative with Scraps: Use vegetable scraps (like onion peels, carrot tops, and celery leaves) to make homemade vegetable broth.
